# Adjacent Efforts

This project is user-first and Apple-specific. It is not a substitute for broader digital rights, antitrust, repair, interoperability, privacy, or free-software work.

Related efforts and organizations include:

- [Marco Arment's "A letter to John Ternus"](https://marco.org/2026/04/01/letter-to-john-ternus), discussed on [ATP 686](https://atp.fm/686), for the broader user-respect framing around Apple and personal computing.
- [Coalition for App Fairness](https://appfairness.org/our-vision/) for developer-focused App Store principles and competition policy.
- [Electronic Frontier Foundation](https://www.eff.org/) for digital rights, privacy, interoperability, and platform-control advocacy.
- [Free Software Foundation](https://www.fsf.org/) and [Defective by Design](https://www.defectivebydesign.org/) for software freedom and anti-DRM advocacy.
- [Open Web Advocacy](https://open-web-advocacy.org/) for browser engine choice and open-web rights on locked-down platforms.
- [iFixit](https://www.ifixit.com/) and right-to-repair advocates for repairability and ownership of hardware.

The gap this repo tries to fill is narrower:

Apple should make its platforms feel owned by the user again.
